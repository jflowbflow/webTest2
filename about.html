Spyro 2:

This is one of my personal favorites. Great mechanics and narrative for the target audience.

The Witness:

Awesome experience! A metaphor for life with a fun learning curve. Low-poly and perspectives are my favorite kind of art.

Kingdom Hearts (I and II):

Simple while having complex fighting mechanics along with the never ending story.

League of Legends:

Started playing this after Warcraft 3 DotA and thought it was way simpler. The competitiveness makes the game, the mechanics provide interesting interactions to fool around with. I got platinum rank in season 3, which means I'm good!

Halo 3:

So much fun, Campaign, Matchmaking, Custom games, plus Forge (custom maps). This was my first taste of in-game design. I also made a lot of Xbox Live friends.

World of Warcraft:

Wow. The story is pretty heavy, especially when analyzing its macro civilization rhetoric.

Portal (and Portal 2):

I like thinking with portals. The difference between these games is tremendous even though they are both awesome for the same reason.

Flow:

Beautiful, simple, and polished. I liked the title a lot. Jenova Chen's thesis is pretty cool too!

140:

Pinnacle of all music games I have played. Awesome level design that I did a presentation on. Made me feel way better after each challenge than any Guitar Hero song ever did.

Journey:

Another beautiful game with a lot of polish and crazy good systems to get the desired feels.
