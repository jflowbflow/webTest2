MITO: VR

Date: May 2017

Timeframe: 4 Months.

Position: Systems, and Sound Designer

Platform: HTC Vive with Unity

Responsibilities: QA testing, interaction feedback, UX, and sound effects.

Tools: Illustrator for creating documents. Live 9 and Audition sound. Forms for QA data.

 

Exhibited to Ubisoft Montreal and received positive feedback. Available here.

 

MITO: VR is an experimental VR game set in an unknown organic environment, where nothing is created or destroyed, only interacted with. The game is a mix between a mysterious wondrous atmosphere, and gross, disgusting flesh. It is a cinematic experience, with an emphasis on exploration and sightseeing. In the game, you can pick, pull, and peel objects, to progress through this unique environment. Ultimately, making you care and protect your light source as it awakens the swarm within the cave.

Team-size: 9 People

Modscript

Date: March 2017

Timeframe: 10 weeks

Position: Designer, Programmer, Producer

Platform: In-browser WebGL, in JavaScript and Three.js

Responsibilities: Iterative design, bringing it to QA, and making changes.

Tools: Three.js, Node.js, and Github to get it working. Slides for documentation.

 

The game is available to play in-browser, and received a lot of positive feedback and playtime from speed-runners. The game went through a total of five, two-week sprints, and has a small speedrunning community, along with a website.

 

Modscript is an old-school arcade speed-running first person shooter. It is a heavily modified version of the open source game Gorescript, with a target audience of speed-runners. The game went through many different iterations in order to get the desired balance between competitive speed running, and ease of use. During development, I was advised by Patrick Fortier (Gameplay Director at Eidos Montreal). The game is about emulating the arcade FPS era, while making the player feel like a cybernetic speed power house.

SYSTEM: Cyclone

Date: May 2016

Timeframe: 5 weeks.

Position: Systems, Level, and Audio Designer

Platform: Android, PC, with Unreal

Responsibilities: Steering the ship with: concepting, design, and iteration.

Tools: Illustrator for creating documents. Live 9 and Excel for music and level design.

 

Check out the team postmortem at: systemcyclone.com

 

SYSTEM: Cyclone was an ambitious project with an easily scalable idea.  The idea was a 3D musical arcade game for mobile. As this was my first 3D game, and a musical one, the challenge for me was to visualize and make levels along with the soundtrack.

 

I started out gathering information about music games to see how we could differentiate ourselves in the market. With a considerable amount of research, I then started to document the system of this eight lane endless runner. After finalizing how the system was going to work, I began laying out levels in Excel while composing them in Ableton. Every meeting I gave feedback to my team to improve visual feedback, level implementation, and marketing, along with making test plans and attending QA to see what play-testers did.

 

The game was exhibited at Gamefest 2016 and received compliments from many attendees and professors.

Team-size: 5 People

The Eightfold Path

Date: April 2016

Timeframe: 4 weeks.

Position: Systems, and Sound

Platform: Android, PC, with Unity

Responsibilities: Research and concepting, while designing the puzzle mechanics.

Tools: Illustrator for concepting. Live 9 for music. Whiteboards for lots of scrapped ideas.

 

The Eightfold Path is an experimental educational game that teaches Buddhist philosophy through gameplay. My specific role on this project was to research Buddhism in order to align the mindfulness, tranquility, and balance of Buddhist life into the game. The core idea is the Dharma Wheel, which the player, like the Buddha, will spin to create harmony in the universe by balancing their chakras. Our earliest goal was to make a game without a losing condition, and instead after each lifetime your most prominent chakra color would be displayed in the center. To win, the player would balance all the chakra colors in order to reach Nirvana and see their colorful portrait.

 

As far as design choices go, the player didn't have a lot of choices to influence the randomness of the game. We went through a few iterations on how to balance the games difficulty. Development concluded with a well polished prototype playable on Android phones.

Team-size: 5 People

Ksagi!

Date: February 2016

Timeframe: 4 weeks.

Position: Producer, Scrum Master

Platform: PC, with Unity

Responsibilities: Resolving communication issues, and finalizing releases.

Tools: Redmine for project management, and wiki upkeep.

 

KSAGI! is a head to head turn based fighting game similar to rock paper scissors. 2 players compete with five elemental based spells which combine together to influence the next turns spell effects. The model is a simple 5x5 matrix of effectiveness. The problems that arose were mostly due to the scope and time constraint of the project. The game design is a strategic guessing game within the nonsensical world we attempted to create.

 

This was the first game I made as a producer / product owner. In general I made sure the game had a focus while scheduling and logging meetings, as well as organizing the wiki and asset list.

Team-size: 5 People

Big_Data

Date: January 2017

Timeframe: 2 weeks.

Platform: Android, using Unity

Responsibilities: Conception, Creation, and publishing.

 

Big_Data is a mobile puzzle game with a real time matching mechanic. You are running a homebrew program based on old school touchscreen mods and hacking programs, complete with a functional virtual button display. The difficulty of making this game came from the iterations I went through to make the virtual controls work with the challenge of the game. From early on I didn't want to just make a standard matching game and a virtual UI was a cost effective solution to this.

 

Making a game with buttons on a touchscreen is problematic for almost all of the affordances a touchscreen puzzle game brings to the table. However, I think the virtual display does enhance the level of attention and hard thinking you have to perform within the difficulty and context of the games overall challenge. It was an interesting project that I worked on over winter break. It is currently available on the Google Play store for free!

Team-size: Solo

Lich Lord's Descent

Date: August 2017

Timeframe: 2 weeks.

Platform: PC, using Unity

Responsibilities: Design, Conception, Creation, and Distributing

 

Made for a self proposed design challenge to design a champion for Battlerite. The goal was to create a ranged champion that was balanced statistically, using data from other champions in Battlerite. The idea was to create a design that provides a unique and more diverse direction for the game, while also being familiar and fitting in with the games current state of mechanics. The prototype displays how the theoretical champion would work in game, rather than just explaining the Lich Lord's abilities in plain text.

 

The prototype is available to download on itch.io where you can view my developer commentary about how I went about balancing the champion, and my personal postmortem.

Clip Art VR

Date: December 2016

Timeframe: 4 weeks.

Platform: Oculus Gear VR, using Unity

Responsibilities: Working with a graphic designer, programming, and testing in VR.

It is currently being reviewed for publishing on the Oculus Store.

 

Clip Art VR is an experimental art gallery in Virtual Reality. It features art by Andrea Cincotta. I wanted the game to reflect the abstractness of her art, through exploration and visuals. The game was made with the intention that the user did not have to view all the art pieces in order to complete it, as it is an art gallery, they can view a few pieces and be done.

 

They were not required to move / float around in the space if they didn't want to. If they did they would be rewarded with scenic views of an 'aquatic' space like gallery. It required players to think outside the box to progress, and often they did not know what to do. However, they reported being hugely satisfied with smiles on their faces when they discovered a new virtual space to explore.

Team-size: 2 People

Astro-Sovereignty

Date: December 2015

Timeframe: 5-6 weeks of prototyping and polish.

Platform: Board game on PC, using Unity

Responsibilities: Creating a paper prototype, balancing rule-sets, and a digital prototype.

 

Asto-Sovereignty is a turn based strategy game of galactic conquest for 3 players. The original concept came from the board game GO, but on an isometric grid. The game's rule sets and win conditions went through several iterations. I made a lot of changes to refocus players, like the addition of resources, and different ship types, as well as fleet formations and an objective based win condition.

 

Ultimately this was my first attempt to balance and create a multiplayer experience and I learned a lot from it. Testing and responding to feedback changed the game experience drastically and made the ideas I wanted in the game increasingly evident. Interestingly enough the complex number of rules I added turned players off at first, but once the game started they were easy to remember. The game received a lot of great remarks on its potential product value, but by the end of the project I felt that there were a lot of tweaks the game needed to fulfill its worth.

Team-size: Solo
